Standard Bank Case Study

2012 - Standard Bank - Multi-Asset Trading Platform

 

The Challenge.

 

Standard Bank is South Africa's largest bank and has extensive operations in 17 African countries. Outside the African continent, the bank operates in a further 16 countries - with a focus on emerging markets.

 

In 2012 Standard Bank intended to secure its position as the world's leading emerging markets bank by working with Caplin to development of a new, multi-asset trading platform that would revolutionize the way the bank delivers its electronic trading capabilities, across various product lines, to its customers and other end-users.

 

The vision was to implement a platform that will unify access to trading functionality across multiple business silos - thereby creating a single interface for trading in a wide range of different instruments.

Standard Bank's emerging market focus means that it is particularly important to select a technology that can be accessed from almost anywhere and run over networks that will vary considerably in terms of speed and resilience.

 

Standard Bank wanted unlimited freedom to innovate in terms of the functionality and content that it delivers to its various types of customer. They also wanted a highly competitive new online product that would considerably strengthen its appeal to customers, existing and potential - both within its local African markets and globally.

 

Standard Bank expressed a strong preference for a solution which would run natively in a web browser (preferably without reliance on plug-ins) and which would cope with Internet connections that may be slow and unreliable. This was considered crucial because it would maximise the accessibility of the new offering, whilst minimising the cost of setting up new customers and supporting existing ones.

 

 

The Solution.

 

Caplin proposed that Standard Bank implement FX Motif and Caplin Trader, a rich Internet application (RIA) framework built using Caplin Platform, a proven single dealer platform (SDP) architecture that is successfully used by banks to deliver trading in FX and other asset types direct to their customers via the Internet.

 

The project will pull in the expertise of Caplin Professional Services and be implemented by a 12-person combined Caplin/Standard Bank team. FX and Precious Metals are planned to be the first asset classes of the multi-asset portal to be launched during 2013 and will be initially used for spot, forwards, swaps and orders

 

 

The Result.

 

Since using Caplin Trader Standard Bank has seen impressive results in on-boarding new customers and because of a better order management system it has seen user engagement go up.

 

Because it was delivered as a managed project it freed Standard Bank staff to work on other projects, allowing the bank to deliver multiple projects at once.

 

The decision to install Caplin Trader has also been received positively by Management “The growth of the internet in Africa determined that online was the only way to go, but we needed to be able to handle the diverse infrastructure capabilities of developing and developed markets," says James Nicolson, head of e-commerce IT in the corporate and investment banking division of Standard Bank. "We evaluated several technologies but decided on Caplin Trader as it was unique in offering a cost-effective solution that lets us build a high-performance robust single-dealer platform.”
